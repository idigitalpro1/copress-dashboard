# What Comes Next: The Road From Arraignment to Verdict

**OPINION / EXPLAINER** — A 79-count indictment is a starting line, not a finish line. For readers who want to follow this case past the headline, here is the road ahead — and the off-ramps that exist at every mile.

**Arraignment and pleas.** The defendants appear, hear the charges, and enter pleas. RCI has signaled it will fight, calling the charges baseless.

**Discovery.** Both sides exchange evidence. This is where the prosecution's "best texts" meet context, and where the defense gets to test how the government built its theory.

**Pretrial motions.** Defense lawyers can move to dismiss counts, suppress evidence, or sever defendants. Long indictments often get shorter here. The dramatic count total can shrink well before a jury ever sees it.

**Resolution or trial.** Many cases settle. If this one does not, the government must prove every element of every surviving count beyond a reasonable doubt, before a jury, with the defense fully able to answer. And a verdict is still not the end — there are appeals.

We lay this out for one reason: to keep expectations honest. Anyone telling you today how this ends is guessing. The only thing the law has actually established so far is that charges have been filed and denied. Everything else is still to be proven. We'll cover each step as it comes — fairly, and without pretending the ending is already written.
