# Rick's / RCI — New York Case: Opinion Series (Editor's Working Notes)

**Status:** DRAFT for publisher review. Not for publication until legal review is complete.
**Publisher:** Patrick Henry Sweeney
**Subject:** *People of the State of New York v. Eric Langan et al.* — 79-count indictment unsealed Sept. 16, 2025 (NY Attorney General Letitia James).

## Hard rules baked into every article in this series
1. Every count is described as an **allegation**. The defendants are **presumed innocent unless and until proven guilty.**
2. The series is labeled **OPINION / EDITORIAL**. Opinion is protected; false statements of fact are not.
3. **No fabricated quotes.** The only direct quotes used are real and sourced: the AG's press release and RCI Hospitality's published statement.
4. Where the indictment's allegations are described, they are attributed to the AG ("the indictment alleges," "prosecutors say").
5. RCI's denial is included for fairness in every piece that recounts the allegations.

## Sourced facts used (verify before publishing)
- NY AG press release, Sept. 16, 2025: 79 counts; RCI Hospitality Holdings + 5 executives (Eric Langan, Bradley Chhay, Ahmed "Ed" Anakar, Shaun Kevlin, Timothy Winata) + 3 Manhattan clubs (Rick's Cabaret, Vivid Cabaret, Hoops Cabaret); alleged bribery of a NY tax auditor to underpay ~$8M sales tax on "Dance Dollars," 2010–2024; top counts carry 8⅓–25 years.
- RCI Hospitality statement (Sept. 16, 2025): "...deny the allegations and will take all necessary action to defend themselves against these overreaching charges... these indictments contain only allegations, which we believe are baseless." Company says its policy is to pay "all legitimate and non-contested taxes"; all three Manhattan clubs remain open.
- Lou Bunch Day: honors Louisa "Lou" Bunch, Central City's last madam (brothel on Pine St. to 1914; converted it to a hospital during a TB epidemic). Festival since 1974; bed races down Main Street the second weekend of June.
