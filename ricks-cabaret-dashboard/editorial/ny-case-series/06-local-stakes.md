# Local Stakes: The Corridor Has Skin in This Game

**OPINION** — The indictment is a New York story, but the brand on the marquee is a Central City story. Rick's operation on Main Street is part of the late-night economy of the Black Hawk / Central City corridor — dinner, drinks and entertainment that keep visitors in town after the casino tables close, and that keep local people employed.

That is a fair thing to weigh while the legal process plays out in another state. A national headline about a parent company can wash over a local operation that had nothing to do with a Manhattan audit, frightening customers and rattling workers who simply show up and do their jobs. The corporate defendants named in New York are the three Manhattan clubs; the company says all of them remain open and operating.

We make a distinction the headlines rarely do: a corporation and its executives are facing allegations in New York; the people pouring drinks and seating tables in Central City are facing a Tuesday shift. The first is a matter for the courts. The second is a matter for us — neighbors, customers, and a local press that ought to be careful about turning an out-of-state indictment into a local layoff.

Support due process. Support the presumption of innocence. And support the corridor's workers, whose paychecks should not be collateral damage in a case that has not been tried.
