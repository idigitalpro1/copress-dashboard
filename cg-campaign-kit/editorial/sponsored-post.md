In 1991, three struggling Colorado mountain towns made a bet on themselves. They voted to legalize limited casino gaming — and 35 years later, that bet has paid off to the tune of $1.8 billion in annual gaming revenue, thousands of jobs, and the preservation of some of the most historically significant architecture in the American West.

Black Hawk, Central City, and Cripple Creek are unlike any other gaming destinations in the country. They are not Las Vegas. They are not Atlantic City. They are Victorian mining towns perched high in the Rocky Mountains, where the history of the American West is written in the facades of 19th-century opera houses, assay offices, and saloons — and where, behind those facades, you'll find some of the most competitive casino floors in the region.

## Black Hawk: Colorado's Casino Capital

Black Hawk sits at 8,042 feet in Clear Creek Canyon, 40 miles west of Denver. It is the largest of Colorado's three gaming towns by revenue, and it is home to some of the most impressive casino resort properties outside of Nevada and New Jersey.

The transformation of Black Hawk since 1993 has been extraordinary. What was once a quiet mountain community of a few hundred residents is now a corridor of resort casinos, hotel towers, and parking structures that generates hundreds of millions of dollars in gaming revenue annually. The town's permanent population is still small — but on any given weekend, tens of thousands of visitors make the drive up Clear Creek Canyon.

**Ameristar Casino Resort Black Hawk** is the flagship property — a full-service resort with over 1,600 slot machines, 40 table games, a hotel, multiple restaurants, and a spa. It is the largest casino in Colorado by square footage and consistently ranks among the top gaming properties in the Mountain West.

The **Isle Casino Hotel Black Hawk** and **Monarch Casino Resort Spa** are the other major resort properties, each offering hotel accommodations, multiple dining options, and full casino floors. The **Golden Gates Casino**, **Golden Gulch Casino**, and **Saratoga Casino** round out the Black Hawk portfolio with smaller, more traditional casino experiences.

For players, Black Hawk offers the widest selection of games, the highest table limits, and the most amenities of any Colorado gaming destination. For operators, it is the most competitive market in the state.

## Central City: Where Colorado Gaming Was Born

Central City is two miles from Black Hawk, connected by a winding mountain road that follows the original wagon trail. It is smaller, quieter, and arguably more historically significant than its neighbor.

Central City was once called "the richest square mile on earth." The gold discovered in Gregory Gulch in 1859 made it the most important city between St. Louis and San Francisco. The **Central City Opera House**, built in 1878 and still operating today, is a testament to the wealth and ambition of the Victorian era. The **Teller House**, built in 1872 as a luxury hotel for visiting dignitaries, is home to the famous "Face on the Barroom Floor" — a painted portrait that has captivated visitors for over a century.

Today, Central City has five licensed casinos, including the **Century Casino Central City**, the **Dostal Alley Brewpub & Casino**, and the **Famous Bonanza Casino**. The gaming floors are smaller than Black Hawk's resort properties, but the historic setting is unmatched. Walking through Central City is walking through the history of the American West.

The Central City Parkway, completed in 2004, provides direct highway access from I-70 and has made Central City significantly more accessible to Denver-area visitors.

## Cripple Creek: The Last Great Gold Rush Town

Cripple Creek is different from Black Hawk and Central City. It sits on the southwestern slope of Pikes Peak, at an elevation of 9,494 feet — higher than any other Colorado gaming town. It is reached by a winding mountain road from Colorado Springs or by the historic Phantom Canyon Road from Cañon City.

The town was born in 1890, when Bob Womack discovered gold on the slopes of Pikes Peak. The Cripple Creek gold rush was the last great gold rush in Colorado history, and the mines produced an estimated $800 million in gold before they went quiet in the early 20th century. The town nearly died — but gaming brought it back.

Cripple Creek has eight licensed casinos, including the **Bronco Billy's Casino**, **Double Eagle Hotel & Casino**, **Midnight Rose Hotel & Casino**, and **Wildwood Casino**. The town has maintained more of its historic character than Black Hawk — the Victorian storefronts, the narrow streets, and the mountain setting create an atmosphere that is genuinely unlike anywhere else in Colorado gaming.

The **Cripple Creek Heritage Center** and the **Mollie Kathleen Gold Mine** offer historical context for visitors who want to understand the town's remarkable story.

## Colorado Sports Betting: The New Frontier

In May 2020, Colorado became one of the first states to launch legal online sports betting. Amendment 77, approved by voters in November 2019, authorized the Colorado Division of Gaming to license online sportsbook operators — and the launch coincided, somewhat chaotically, with the COVID-19 pandemic shutting down every sport.

Today, Colorado has 13 licensed online sportsbooks, including DraftKings, FanDuel, BetMGM, Caesars, BetRivers, Betway, and seven additional operators.
