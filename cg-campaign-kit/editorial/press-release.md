## Colorado Gambler Relaunches as Definitive Digital Authority on Colorado Casino Gaming After 35 Years of Print Publishing

*Patrick Sweeney's landmark Colorado gaming publication expands to full digital platform with casino directory, sportsbook promo tracker, and historic podcast archive*

---

**DENVER, CO — February 26, 2026** — The Colorado Gambler, the publication that has covered Colorado casino gaming since the day Amendment 4 legalized limited gambling in 1991, today announced the relaunch of its digital platform at coloradogambler.com. The redesigned site brings 35 years of editorial authority to a modern digital format, featuring a comprehensive casino directory, a real-time Colorado sportsbook promo tracker, a historic podcast series, and a verified casino listing program for operators.

Founded by Patrick Sweeney in 1991 — the same year Colorado voters approved limited gaming in Black Hawk, Central City, and Cripple Creek — the Colorado Gambler has published more than 1,000 issues covering every casino opening, legislative development, and gaming story in Colorado's three mountain gaming towns. The publication's archive represents the most comprehensive record of Colorado casino history in existence.

"The Colorado Gambler was born with Colorado gaming," said Patrick Sweeney, founder and publisher. "We were there when the first casinos opened in 1993, when the bet limits were raised in 2001, when Ameristar opened in Black Hawk, and when sports betting launched in 2020. This relaunch is about making 35 years of that knowledge accessible to every player and operator in Colorado."

### Platform Features

The relaunched coloradogambler.com includes four primary sections:

**Casino Guide** — A complete directory of all 22 licensed casinos across Black Hawk, Central City, and Cripple Creek, with detailed profiles, amenity information, gaming floor details, and direct links to each property's current promotions. Casino operators can claim and manage their listings through the site's new verified listing program.

**Colorado Sportsbook Promos** — A real-time tracker of welcome bonuses, promo codes, and deposit match offers from all 13 licensed Colorado online sportsbooks, including DraftKings, FanDuel, BetMGM, Caesars, and nine additional operators. The tracker is updated weekly to reflect current offers.

**Historic Podcasts** — An original audio series covering Colorado gaming history, from the 1991 ballot initiative that created the industry to the first year of legal sports betting. Season 1 features eight episodes covering Black Hawk, Central City, Cripple Creek, and the legislative history of Colorado gaming.

**Back Issues Archive** — A browsable digital archive of Colorado Gambler back issues dating to 1991, available to registered users. The archive includes more than 35 years of editorial coverage, casino reviews, and gaming industry reporting.

### Verified Casino Listing Program

The Colorado Gambler is now accepting applications from licensed casino operators in all three gaming towns for its verified listing program. Verified operators receive enhanced directory profiles, direct promotional links, the Colorado Gambler Verified badge, and access to the publication's brand kit. Applications are available at coloradogambler.com/claim-listing.

### About Colorado Gaming

Colorado's three gaming towns — Black Hawk, Central City, and Cripple Creek — collectively generate approximately $1.8 billion in annual gaming revenue and support thousands of jobs across the state. The industry was established by Amendment 4, approved by Colorado voters in November 1991, which authorized limited stakes gaming in the three historic mining communities. Colorado legalized online sports betting in May 2020 through Amendment 77, adding 13 licensed sportsbook operators to the state's gaming landscape.
