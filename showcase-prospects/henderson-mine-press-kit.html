Henderson Mine

Core angle: Henderson Mine is not just a mine story. It is a Colorado mountain community story about jobs, molybdenum, water, safety, long-term operations, local partnerships, and what comes next for Clear Creek and Grand counties.

Working headline: Inside Henderson Mine: The Colorado Molybdenum Operation Connecting Work, Water, and Mountain Communities.

Nut graf: Henderson Mine, operated by Climax Molybdenum Company, is an underground molybdenum operation near Empire, Colorado, with its mill across the Continental Divide in Grand County. The mine has operated since 1976, and its mine-and-mill system is connected by a long conveyor route that includes a tunnel beneath the Continental Divide.

Verified Quick Facts

  • Henderson Mine is operated by Climax Molybdenum Company, a Freeport-McMoRan company.
  • The mine is near Empire, Georgetown, and Idaho Springs; the Henderson mill site is in Grand County.
  • The mine and mill are separated by the Continental Divide and connected by a 10-mile conveyor tunnel plus an additional surface conveyor.
  • Public materials describe the operation as a large block-cave underground mining complex feeding a concentrator.
  • Q1 2026 community materials listed approximately 425 employees and 80 contractors.
  • Public materials say the Northwest panel supports the current life-of-mine plan through 2038.

History Pack

  • 1967: Henderson ore body development began.
  • 1976: First ore produced.
  • 1999: Conveyor system conversion completed.
  • 2007: Freeport-McMoRan acquired Phelps Dodge.
  • 2025: Public materials reported strongest safety performance since 2018.
  • 2038: Current public life-of-mine plan reference.

Current Story Themes

Jobs and stability

Ask what Henderson jobs mean for Empire, Georgetown, Idaho Springs, Kremmling, Grand County, and Clear Creek County.

Water and environment

Ask about the mill water treatment plant, low snowpack, dust suppression, wildfire planning, and molybdenum water-quality standards.

Community partnerships

Ask how the Community Investment Fund and Community Partnership Panel support local priorities in Grand and Clear Creek Counties.
